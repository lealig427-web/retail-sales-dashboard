# Retail Sales Dashboard — Excel
 
## Overview
An end-to-end retail analytics project built in Microsoft Excel using the [Superstore Dataset](https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/vivek468/superstore-dataset-final) from Kaggle. The dashboard analyzes ~10,000 orders across categories, regions, and time to surface actionable business insights on revenue, profitability, and product mix.
 
## Tools Used
- Microsoft Excel — PivotTables, PivotCharts, Slicers, conditional formatting, calculated fields
## Process
1. **ETL** — Loaded raw CSV, corrected data types, built calculated columns (Profit Margin %)
2. **PivotTables** — Summarized sales and profit by category, sub-category, region, and month
3. **Dashboard** — Single-page layout with 4 visuals, KPI summary row, month slicer, and written insight boxes per chart
## Key Findings
- **Technology dominates:** ~$200K in Q1 sales — a ~$60K gap above Furniture, signaling clear category skew toward higher-value segments
- **Copiers and Binders drive profitability:** The top 2 sub-categories account for a disproportionate share of total profit; most sub-categories contribute minimally
- **West and East lead, Central underperforms:** West (~$725K sales, ~$105K profit) and East (~$670K, ~$90K profit) drive the majority of revenue; Central generates ~$500K in sales but only ~$40K profit — the weakest margin efficiency of any region

## How to Use
1. Download `superstore_clean.xlsx`
2. Open in Microsoft Excel (2016 or later)
3. Use the **Month slicer** to filter all charts by time period
4. Insight boxes below/beside each chart explain the key takeaway
